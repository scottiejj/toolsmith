### 1. Competition Overview
The "obesity_risks" competition is part of the Kaggle Playground Series, where the primary goal is to predict the risk of obesity in individuals, which is associated with cardiovascular diseases. Participants are challenged to use various factors to classify individuals into different obesity risk categories. The competition runs from February 1, 2024, to March 1, 2024, and submissions will be evaluated based on accuracy.

### 2. Files
The competition provides three essential files:
- **train.csv**: This file contains the training dataset, which includes various features related to individuals' demographics and lifestyle choices. The target variable is `NObeyesdad`, indicating obesity classification.
  
- **test.csv**: This file consists of the test dataset for which participants must predict the target variable `NObeyesdad`.
  
- **sample_submission.csv**: A template for the submission file demonstrating the required format for the predictions.

### 3. Problem Definition
The problem requires participants to predict the obesity classification of individuals based on several features. The target variable, `NObeyesdad`, includes multiple classes representing different levels of obesity. The objective is to develop a model that accurately predicts these classes for the test dataset.

### 4. Data Information
#### 4.1 Data Type
- **ID type**
  - `id`: A unique identifier for each data point (not used in model training).
  
- **Numerical type**
  - `Age`: Age of the individual (in years).
  - `Height`: Height (in meters).
  - `Weight`: Weight (in kilograms).
  - `CH2O`: Daily water intake (liters).
  - `FAF`: Frequency of physical activity.
  - `TUE`: Time spent exercising (in hours).
  - `CALC`: Daily calcium intake (grams).

- **Categorical type**
  - `Gender`: Gender of the individual (Male/Female).
  - `family_history_with_overweight`: Family history of overweight (yes/no).
  - `FAVC`: Food intake frequency (yes/no).
  - `FCVC`: Frequency of vegetable consumption (numerical scale).
  - `NCP`: Number of meals consumed per day (numerical scale).
  - `CAEC`: Consumption of food between meals (categorical).
  - `SMOKE`: Smoking status (yes/no).
  - `SCC`: Self-calculated calories (yes/no).
  - `MTRANS`: Means of transportation (categorical).
  - `NObeyesdad`: Level of obesity (target variable).

- **Datetime type**
  - None.

#### 4.2 Detailed Data Description
The dataset is structured into two segments: training and testing. It captures demographic and lifestyle information, such as age, gender, height, weight, dietary habits, and physical activities. The target variable classifies individuals into obesity levels, ranging from "Insufficient Weight" to "Obesity Type III." Observations include:
- Predominantly young adults (ages 18-32).
- Variations in height and weight.
- Significant prevalence of family history related to overweight.
- Engagement in physical activities varies among individuals.
- Common modes of transportation include public transport and automobiles.

### 5. Target Variable
The target variable, `NObeyesdad`, categorizes obesity levels into the following classes:
- "Insufficient Weight"
- "Normal Weight"
- "Overweight Level I"
- "Overweight Level II"
- "Obesity Type I"
- "Obesity Type II"
- "Obesity Type III"

### 6. Evaluation Metrics
Submissions will be evaluated using the accuracy score, which measures the proportion of correctly predicted classifications out of the total predictions made.

### 7. Submission Format

- `NObeyesdad`: Predicted obesity classification.
